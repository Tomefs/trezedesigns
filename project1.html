Tomé Simões

Graphic Designer

Kaija's Flowers


Project created as part of the Generative Design course in the Design and Multimedia Master's Degree program at the University of Coimbra


This sketch in Processing creates a dynamic audiovisual composition that combines real-time video , ASCII art, and graphic animations.


The basis of the project are videos of plants, chosen specifically for their dark backgrounds, which are loaded and played in a continuous loop, with the aim of highlighting the colors of the natural elements, filters, and lighting effects. The application continuously generates small animated rectangles, whose position, size, and effects are random within a given range, but conditioned by the color of the video image, each displaying an excerpt from the original video at that position applying graphic effects such as dilation, erosion, color inversion, or posterization.


Inspired by the work and elements that inspired composer Kaija Saariaho, where natural elements, color, light, visual arts and technology merge into a single collection of unique and constantly changing artistic objects that give image to the artist's sound compositions.
