Laini Abernathy the Exhibition


Group project created as part of the Design and Multimedia Workshop course in the Design and Multimedia Master's Degree program at the University of Coimbra


For this project we were tasked with the design and curation of a fictional exhibition about a designer that would be randomly assigned. We got Laini Abernathy. The goal was to create a cohesive visual identity between inspired by the designers history and work all the elements that comprised the exhibition, including the exhibition space, posters, flyers, merchandise, and a 3D model of the exhibition space.


Born in a time when the world refused to see the full color and complexity of Black life, Abernathy designed a future in full view. She made album covers that didn’t just package sound they extended it. She framed jazz not as a genre, but as a political language. Through type, color, rhythm, and form, she visualized a liberation that was both radical and intimate.


The identity for this exhibition draws directly from her legacy. Inspired by Abernathy’s bold uses of geometric composition, kinetic typography, and political symbolism, the visual system of this book pays tribute to her refusal of neutrality. Color becomes a voice. Type becomes a gesture. Every layout echoes the improvisational structure of jazz: a controlled freedom, alive with tension, rhythm, and breath.
